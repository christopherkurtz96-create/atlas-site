Mobile Home Removal Cost: Quick Overview

Mobile home demolition in Mid-Missouri typically costs between $3,000 and $8,000, depending primarily on whether you have a single-wide or double-wide unit. Here's the breakdown:

Single-Wide

$3,000 - $5,000
  • 600-900 sq ft typical
  • 1-2 days to complete
  • Includes skirting removal
  • Basic site cleanup included

Double-Wide

$5,000 - $8,000
  • 1,000-1,800 sq ft typical
  • 2-3 days to complete
  • More material to haul
  • Often has larger decks

These prices include the complete demolition, debris removal, hauling to disposal facilities, and basic site cleanup. Your actual cost depends on several factors we'll cover below.

What Affects Mobile Home Demolition Cost?

Several factors influence your final mobile home removal cost in Missouri:

Size and Type

The biggest cost factor is simply the size of the unit. Single-wide trailers (14-18 feet wide) require less labor and generate less debris than double-wide units. Triple-wide or modular homes cost $7,000-$10,000+ due to their larger size.

Condition and Contents

An empty mobile home in reasonable condition is straightforward to demolish. However, costs increase if:

  • The unit is full of belongings that need removal
  • Significant water damage has caused structural rot
  • Fire damage is present
  • The trailer has collapsed or is severely deteriorated

Site Accessibility

Properties with easy access for equipment keep costs down. Factors that can increase your price:

  • Long or narrow driveways
  • Steep or uneven terrain
  • Trees or structures blocking access
  • Soft ground conditions (common in spring)

Attached Structures

Most mobile homes have additions that need removal too:

Structure Additional Cost
Wood deck or porch $300 - $800
Concrete steps or pad $200 - $500
Metal carport or awning $300 - $600
Storage shed (small) $300 - $800

What's Included in Mobile Home Removal?

When you hire a professional for mobile home demolition in Columbia, MO, the service should include:

  • Complete structure demolition - Breaking down the entire mobile home
  • Skirting removal - Removing metal, vinyl, or wood skirting
  • Foundation cleanup - Removing blocks, piers, and runners
  • Debris hauling - Loading and transporting all materials
  • Proper disposal - Taking debris to licensed facilities
  • Basic site cleanup - Raking and leveling the area

Items typically quoted separately include deck removal, concrete work, septic tank abandonment, and extensive site grading for new construction.

The Mobile Home Removal Process

Here's what to expect when you schedule mobile home demolition:

  1. Site assessment - We visit your property to evaluate the mobile home, access, and any complications
  2. Utility disconnection - Electric, gas, water, and sewer must be disconnected before demolition (often coordinated by the homeowner)
  3. Permits (if required) - We help navigate local permit requirements
  4. Demolition - Systematic breakdown of the structure
  5. Loading and hauling - Materials are loaded into trucks and hauled to disposal
  6. Site cleanup - Foundation removal and basic grading

Most single-wide removals are completed in 1-2 days. Double-wide units typically take 2-3 days.

Permit Requirements in Mid-Missouri

Permit requirements for mobile home demolition vary by location:

City of Columbia: A demolition permit is required within city limits. Contact Building & Site Development at 573-874-7474.

Boone County (unincorporated): No county demolition permit is typically required, but you still need proper utility disconnections.

Asbestos consideration: Mobile homes built before 1980 may contain asbestos in flooring, siding, or insulation. Missouri DNR requires inspection before demolition if asbestos is suspected.

Your demolition contractor can help you understand what's required for your specific location.

Why Professional Removal Beats DIY

While it might be tempting to tear down an old trailer yourself, professional mobile home demolition is worth the investment:

  • Safety - Mobile homes contain electrical wiring, gas lines, and potentially hazardous materials
  • Equipment - Proper demolition requires excavators and trucks you'd need to rent
  • Disposal - Landfills charge by weight, and you'll need multiple trips with a trailer
  • Time - What takes professionals 1-2 days could take you weeks of weekends
  • Liability - Professional contractors carry insurance for property damage and injuries

When you factor in equipment rental, disposal fees, and your time, DIY removal often costs nearly as much as hiring professionals—without the efficiency or safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does mobile home removal take?

Single-wide mobile homes typically take 1-2 days to remove completely. Double-wide units take 2-3 days. This includes demolition, hauling, and basic site cleanup.

Do you remove everything, including the foundation?

Yes, our mobile home removal service includes removing the skirting, blocks, piers, and runners. We leave the site clean and level.

What if my mobile home is full of stuff?

We can remove contents as part of the demolition, though this adds to the cost depending on volume. For significant cleanouts, you may want to handle personal items and valuables beforehand.

Can the mobile home be moved instead of demolished?

In some cases, yes—but it's often not cost-effective. Moving a mobile home requires specialized transport, permits, and the unit must be in good enough condition to survive the move. For older or damaged trailers, demolition is usually the better option.

What about the septic tank?

Septic tank abandonment is typically quoted separately. The tank needs to be pumped, then either removed or properly filled and capped according to health department requirements.
